Services we provide

Individual Tax Returns

VJC prepares individual tax return workpapers covering income labels, deductions, offsets, rebates, capital gains and supporting schedules. We review prior-year information but focus on current-year movements, source documents and new tax positions.

Company Tax Returns

Our team prepares company tax return workpapers, tax reconciliation statements, franking account schedules, depreciation workings and Division 7A support where applicable. We also assist with complex year-end tax adjustments and return-ready schedules.

Trust Tax Returns

VJC supports trust tax return preparation, including income distribution workings, beneficiary allocation schedules, tax reconciliation, capital gains treatment and supporting notes. We help ensure trust files are properly documented before manager or partner review.

Partnership Tax Returns

We prepare partnership tax return workpapers covering income, deductions, partner distributions, capital account movements and reconciliation schedules. Queries are documented clearly so unusual movements can be reviewed before finalisation.

SMSF Annual Returns

VJC assists with Self-Managed Superannuation Fund annual return preparation, including member balances, pension schedules, investment income, capital gains, tax components and supporting workpapers. Work is prepared for review within your SMSF workflow.

Payroll Tax and FBT Returns

We support payroll tax return preparation through wage reconciliations, threshold review, state-based payroll summaries and supporting schedules. Employee benefit schedules, reportable fringe benefits amounts with the full workpaper the ATO would ask for.

VJC's Smartsourcing Approach

Tax compliance is not a copy-forward exercise.

VJC refers to prior-year workpapers, but every file is reviewed for current-year movement, new transactions, changed tax treatment and missing support. Our team prepares structured workpapers, maintains a clear document trail and raises queries early. This helps your firm reduce deadline pressure, avoid unnecessary deferrals and keep tax files moving through review without last-minute rebuilding.
